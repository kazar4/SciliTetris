Short video of our first soldering session

Josh makes progress on the ESP Modules!

The first part of our LED creating process. We soldered the LED strip to a new wire so it can be crimped. This used to take us 5+ min each time but by the end we had gotten very efficient at it

The second part of our LED creating process, we take the new LED strip with a wire and crimp on a connector This is so we can plug it into our ESP Module. This process also used to take 5+ min and we'd often mess up the crimp. After this step we wrap the solder area on the strip with electrical tape and pack them up for future assembly on the Scili

An early test after creating our Admin website and a simulation website on the left side. The admin website lets us assign websockets to coordinates on the Scili. In this case the simulation has a websocket for each pixel which matches what it would be like when we have 55 ESP modules on the Scili

After receiving our PCB for our ESP module this was our first test using the firmware to control two LED strips through it

Our first test on a window using ESPs on breadboards (we had not made the PCB yet), tested in Page Rob

First test of our admin software on a breadboard in my room

May 15th, After testing several LED arrangements on the 7th floor, Kazen and David find an arrangement that looks good. This was our first confirmation that the LEDs could look nice on the Scili, and was a big relief since our showcase date was in about 10 days and our past tests had not looked that good
